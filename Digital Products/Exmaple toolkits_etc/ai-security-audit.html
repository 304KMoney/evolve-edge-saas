The AI Governance Problem

AI adoption is outpacing security and compliance programs across every industry. Employees are already using AI tools — often without formal oversight, procurement approval, or governance policies in place. The result is a dangerous gap between capability and control.

Section 02

The Hidden Risks of AI Adoption

Across industries, uncontrolled AI usage introduces sector-specific vulnerabilities. Understanding your industry's unique risk profile is the first step toward meaningful governance.

⚖️
Legal & Compliance
Attorneys submitting privileged communications and confidential case materials into AI drafting tools expose client data to external cloud systems without client consent or bar compliance review.
Privilege at Risk
💰
Financial Services
Analysts uploading transaction records, proprietary financial models, and M&A data into AI reporting tools risk leaking material non-public information and trade secrets.
MNPI Exposure
🏥
Healthcare
Processing protected patient information (PHI) through consumer-grade AI systems violates HIPAA and may trigger mandatory breach notification requirements and OCR audits.
HIPAA Violation
👨‍💻
Software Development
Developers pasting proprietary source code, API keys, credentials, or architecture details into AI coding assistants risk permanent exposure through model training datasets.
IP Leakage
🔍
The Model Training Problem
Many consumer-grade AI tools use your inputs to train and improve their models by default. This means your confidential client data, financial models, patient records, or source code can become part of a vendor's training dataset — accessible in some form to other users or future model versions. Always verify your vendor's data usage policy before deployment.

Audit Step 03 — ⏱ ~25 Minutes

Vendor Risk Evaluation

AI platforms must be vetted with the same rigor as any enterprise software. Use the forms below to evaluate each AI vendor against four critical security criteria. Complete one form per vendor.

🛡️
Vendor Evaluation Protocol
For each AI tool identified in Step 1, complete a vendor assessment using the four critical questions below. Focus first on tools with access to high-risk data categories identified in Step 2. A "Fail" on any question requires immediate review of that tool's deployment.
Q1 Does the vendor store user input data? What are their data retention policies?
Q2 Is submitted data used to train or improve the AI model?
Q3 Is data encrypted in transit and at rest?
Q4 What security certifications does the vendor hold?
